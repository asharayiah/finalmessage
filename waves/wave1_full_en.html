Einstein, Locality & the Entanglement Stress Test

Publication date: September 01, 2025 (Asia/Amman)

Put causality and locality back on the table—without revealing proprietary mechanisms.

Executive Summary

Modern loophole-free Bell tests severely constrain traditional local hidden-variable models. But every theorem rests on assumptions. The Local-Causal Entanglement Stress Test (LCEST) performs a clean, falsifiable audit of two pressure points: coincidence timing and measurement independence. We publish a lean, open protocol anyone can run and analyze.

Protocol (public, mechanism-agnostic)

  1. Event-ready source: polarization-entangled photons (SPDC) with fast, switchable analyzers.
  2. Full timetagging: record arrival times and settings for every event; no pre-binning.
  3. Window audit: compute CHSH as a function of coincidence-window width and pairing rule.
  4. Independence audit: rotate setting sources (QRNG/PRNG/human/cosmic) and estimate dependence bounds from the data.
  5. Open analysis: release raw data + scripts; preregister all processing choices.

Falsifiable Signatures

  • Robustness: violations stable across windows/sources → locality-based completions constrained.
  • Fragility: CHSH depends on window/source independence → a local-causal completion gains traction.
  • Timing asymmetry: setting-conditioned skew in arrival-time histograms beyond detector models → hidden structure.
